2026 Designer, researcher, reviewer OpenAI Build Week submission

What I added to an existing research portfolio during OpenAI Build Week, with a clear cutoff, linked commits, and reusable project guides.

Why it began

Most of the portfolio existed before Build Week. I needed to show exactly what changed during the event.

What changed

Paper Constellation

2026 Designer, writer, reviewer Site experiment

A second way to browse my publications: five accepted papers connected by three recurring research threads, with future work kept anonymous.

Why it began

A year-by-year paper list hid the questions that recur across projects.

What changed

I mapped three recurring research questions on desktop; mobile follows the same papers through time instead of shrinking the map.

Intended outcome

Readers can follow one research question across papers instead of reading the bibliography year by year.

Build Rhythm

2026 Designer, builder, reviewer Site experiment

A page for seeing when I build: weekly GitHub bursts, this site's daily token estimate, and one dated lifetime Codex total.

Why it began

The logs had totals, but they did not show when the work arrived in bursts.

What changed

A later dashboard mixed unlike numbers. I split it into weekly code changes, daily site-build estimates, and one dated lifetime total.

Intended outcome

Readers can see when work clustered without mistaking GitHub activity, site-build estimates, and lifetime usage for one metric.

The IKEA Card Experiment

2026 Interaction designer, builder Site experiment

Click a project card, read the full preview in place, then close it without losing your spot in the collection.

Why it began

I wanted project previews to open inside the grid so visitors could keep seeing the other projects and return to the same card.

What changed

The first transition scaled the whole card and squeezed its content. The current version moves the card without scaling its contents and cancels cleanly when another card opens.

Intended outcome

Visitors can sample a project and return to the same place in the collection without a navigation detour.
